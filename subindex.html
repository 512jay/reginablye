Christopher & Dana Reeve Foundation logo

Chief Program and Policy Officer

At the Christopher & Dana Reeve Foundation, I oversee the National Paralysis Resource Center (NPRC), ensuring that individuals living with paralysis have access to vital resources, advocacy, and support.

  • Developing and managing programs that empower people with paralysis and their caregivers.
  • Promoting accessibility and inclusion at all levels, from policy to community engagement.
  • Advocating for equitable access to healthcare, transportation, and employment.
  • Managing a $10 million federal budget to ensure critical programs receive funding.

Back in the Day

Previously, I served in the federal government under three U.S. presidents, working on disability rights policy, accessibility standards, and independent living regulations.

Federal Leadership in Disability Rights

As a Project Officer for the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, I:

  • Drafted congressional letters and disability policies.
  • Managed $25 million in federal contracts for independent living.
  • Oversaw funding and budget allocations at ACL.
  • Participated in Diversity and Inclusion resource groups.

Presidential Appointment

Earlier in my career, I was appointed by President Barack Obama to serve two terms on the U.S. Access Board, where I worked to ensure equal access and accessibility standards for people with disabilities.

"Inclusion is not just about diversity—it’s about ensuring that the built environment, policies, and programs are fully accessible so that people with disabilities can fully participate in all aspects of society."
